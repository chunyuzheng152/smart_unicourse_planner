Computer Science

Computer Science is the study of designing and developing computer systems.
It encompasses programming, data structures, algorithms, and computer networks to create effective IT solutions.

Courses

Computer Science courses usually cover programming, algorithms, data management, computer systems, networks, and software development. Students learn how to design, build, test, and improve computer-based systems.

Course Area What Students Learn Why It Matters
Programming Basic coding, problem solving, variables, functions, and program structure. This is the foundation for most computer science work.
Data Structures and Algorithms Lists, trees, graphs, searching, sorting, and algorithm efficiency. This helps students write faster and more organised programs.
Databases Database design, SQL, data storage, and data management. Most websites and apps need databases to store user and system data.
Computer Networks Internet structure, communication protocols, routing, and data transfer. This helps students understand how computers communicate with each other.
Software Development Project design, testing, debugging, teamwork, and version control. This prepares students for real software projects.
Advanced Topics Artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, web development, and data-related topics. These areas help students choose a future career direction.
For official course information, please check the UWA Computer Science course page .

Skills

Computer Science students develop both technical skills and general problem-solving skills. These skills are useful in software development, data analysis, cybersecurity, and many other technology areas.

Technical Skills
  • Programming with languages such as Python, Java, C, or JavaScript.
  • Understanding algorithms and data structures.
  • Designing and using databases.
  • Building websites and software applications.
  • Understanding computer systems and networks.
  • Testing and debugging code.
Transferable Skills
  • Logical thinking and problem solving.
  • Teamwork in software projects.
  • Clear communication of technical ideas.
  • Project planning and time management.
  • Learning new tools and technologies.
  • Breaking large problems into smaller tasks.
Programming Algorithms Databases Web Development Networks Problem Solving

Careers

Computer Science graduates can work in software companies, technology teams, government departments, banks, consulting firms, and research-related organisations.

Software Developer

Software developers design, build, and maintain applications, systems, and digital tools.

Web Developer

Web developers create websites and web applications using front-end and back-end technologies.

Data Analyst

Data analysts use data to find patterns, support decisions, and create reports or visualisations.

Cybersecurity Analyst

Cybersecurity analysts help protect computer systems, networks, and data from security risks.

Systems Analyst

Systems analysts study business needs and help design suitable technology solutions.

AI Assistant / ML Assistant

Graduates may support artificial intelligence or machine learning projects through coding and data work.

Fees

Tuition fees for Computer Science may vary depending on the university, student status, study load, and selected units. Domestic and international students may also have different fee structures.

Domestic Students

Domestic students may pay fees based on their course structure and government-supported place status. The final cost can change depending on the number of units taken each year.

International Students

International students usually pay annual tuition fees. The amount can vary by university, year of study, and enrolment load.

Tuition fees may change each year. Please use the UWA Tuition Fee Calculator to check the latest estimated cost.

Scholarships

Computer Science students may be able to apply for different types of scholarships. These scholarships can support students based on academic results, financial need, background, leadership, or study area.

Scholarship Type Description
Academic Merit Scholarship For students with strong academic results.
Equity Scholarship For students who need financial or personal support during study.
International Student Scholarship For eligible international students based on university rules.
STEM-related Scholarship For students studying science, technology, engineering, or mathematics areas.
Leadership or Community Scholarship For students who have leadership experience or community contribution.
Scholarship availability and value may change each year. Please check the UWA Scholarships page for current scholarship options.

Admission Requirements

Admission requirements for Computer Science depend on the university and the student's background. Students usually need to meet academic entry requirements and English language requirements.

Academic Background
  • Completion of high school or equivalent qualification.
  • Meeting the required ATAR or equivalent entry score.
  • Relevant mathematics background may be recommended or required.
English Requirement
  • International students usually need to meet English language requirements.
  • Accepted tests may include IELTS, TOEFL, or equivalent tests.
  • The exact score depends on the university.
Pathway Options

Students who do not meet direct entry requirements may consider foundation programs, diploma pathways, or internal transfer options, depending on the university.

Entry requirements may depend on the course and applicant background. Please check the UWA Entry Standards page for official entry information.
