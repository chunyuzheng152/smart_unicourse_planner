Data Science

Data Science focuses on collecting, analysing, and interpreting data.
It uses statistics, programming, and machine learning to support decision-making.

Courses

Data Science focuses on collecting, processing, analysing, and presenting data. Students learn how to use programming, statistics, databases, and machine learning to solve real-world problems.

Course Area What Students Learn Why It Matters
Programming for Data Science Python, R, data processing, and basic coding for data tasks. Programming helps students clean, analyse, and automate data work.
Statistics Probability, statistical methods, hypothesis testing, and data interpretation. Statistics helps students understand patterns and uncertainty in data.
Databases SQL, database design, data storage, and data management. Data scientists need to collect and manage data from different sources.
Machine Learning Prediction models, classification, clustering, and model evaluation. Machine learning helps students build systems that learn from data.
Data Visualisation Charts, dashboards, reports, and visual storytelling. Visualisation helps users understand data more clearly.
Applied Data Projects Using data to solve business, science, health, or technology problems. Project work helps students connect technical skills with real decisions.
For official course information, please check the UWA Data Science course page .

Skills

Data Science students develop technical skills for working with data, as well as communication skills for explaining data-based findings.

Technical Skills
  • Programming with Python, R, or similar tools.
  • Cleaning and preparing raw data.
  • Using statistics to analyse data.
  • Writing SQL queries and working with databases.
  • Building and testing machine learning models.
  • Creating charts, dashboards, and reports.
Transferable Skills
  • Logical thinking and problem solving.
  • Explaining data results in simple language.
  • Making decisions based on evidence.
  • Working with business or research questions.
  • Teamwork in data projects.
  • Learning new data tools and technologies.
Python Statistics SQL Machine Learning Data Visualisation Problem Solving

Careers

Data Science graduates can work in technology companies, banks, consulting firms, government departments, healthcare organisations, research teams, and many other data-driven workplaces.

Data Analyst

Data analysts clean, analyse, and present data to support business or organisational decisions.

Data Scientist

Data scientists use statistics, programming, and machine learning to find insights from complex data.

Business Analyst

Business analysts connect data findings with business needs and help organisations improve decisions.

Machine Learning Assistant

Machine learning assistants support model building, testing, and data preparation.

Database Analyst

Database analysts help manage, organise, and check data stored in databases.

Research Analyst

Research analysts use data to support academic, government, or industry research projects.

Fees

Tuition fees for Data Science may vary depending on the university, student status, study load, and selected units. Domestic and international students may have different fee structures.

Domestic Students

International Students

International students usually pay annual tuition fees. The amount can vary by course, year of study, and enrolment load.

Admission Requirements

Admission requirements for Data Science depend on the university and the applicant's background. Students usually need to meet academic entry requirements and English language requirements.

Academic Background
  • Completion of high school or equivalent qualification.
  • Meeting the required ATAR or equivalent entry score.
  • Mathematics background may be recommended or required.
  • Some pathway students may enter through diploma or foundation programs.
English Requirement
  • International students usually need to meet English language requirements.
  • Accepted tests may include IELTS, TOEFL, or equivalent tests.
  • The exact score depends on the university and course level.
Pathway Options

Students who do not meet direct entry requirements may consider foundation programs, diploma pathways, or internal transfer options, depending on the university.

Entry requirements may depend on the course and applicant background. Please check the UWA Entry Standards page for official entry information.
